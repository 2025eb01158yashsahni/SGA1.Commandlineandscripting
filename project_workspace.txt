5. EXPLANATION OF HOW PERMISSIONS PROTECT PROJECT DATA
   The implemented permission scheme protects project data through three distinct layers:
   
   * Confidentiality (Isolation): By setting the final octal digit to 0 (---), users who 
     are not the owner and not in the 'project_team' group are strictly prohibited from 
     reading, writing, or traversing the directory. This thwarts internal espionage and 
     accidental data exposure.
     
   * Integrity (Collaborative Controls): Assigning write permissions ('w') to the 
     'project_team' group allows designated engineers to modify, update, and manage files 
     without needing administrative 'sudo' privileges, creating a frictionless workflow.
     
   * Continuity (SetGID Bit enforcement): Standard Linux behavior assigns new files to the 
     primary group of the user who created them. Enforcing the SetGID bit overrides this, 
     making sure all new assets stay group-accessible automatically, preventing "permission 
     denied" obstacles among teammates.
